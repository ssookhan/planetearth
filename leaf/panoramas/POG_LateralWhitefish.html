Jointing
This flat surface is a joint. It's a natural breakage plane which often cuts across bedding, but without displacing it significantly like a fault would.
Where am I?
You are on highway 6, just south of Espanola, Ontario.
Diabase
The blackish greenish rocks are diabase, closely related to basalt. This is the product of mafic magma cooling underground.
Quartzite
The pinkish rocks are quartzite, a metamorphosed sandstone. These particular quartzites belong to the Lorraine Formation which formed in sandy rivers around 2.4 billion years ago.
Bedding
Because these quartzites have been metamorphosed, bedding can be difficult to discern. We can just make it out by looking above this point.
