Kama Bay
The link between geology and topography (what is called 'geomorphology') is especially clear along the north shore of Lake Superior. Spectacular views of flat-topped mountains (called mesas after the Spanish for 'table') capped by flat lying sheets ofigneous rocks can be seen from this overlook of Kama Bay
Sedimentary Strata
The colourful layers of red siltstone, pink and grey sandstone, and grey limestone and chert provide a record of an ancient playa lake environment. These sediments are part of the 1.34 billion year old Sibley Group which is a thick wedge of sediments deposited in an ancient rift basin.

Diabase
This greenish-black rock is diabase, an intrusive version of basalt. It is formed by the cooling of magma. It is about 1.1 billion years old.
