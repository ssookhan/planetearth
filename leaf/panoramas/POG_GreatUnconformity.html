Dip
The angle of these rocks is called a 'dip'. In this case, the dip is caused by folding of metamorphic banded gneisses, and is not of sedimentary origins. However, the clear change in the angle of layering here as opposed to the comparatively flat-lying Paleozoics is a good hint that something important has happened.
Paleozoic Rocks
These more flat-lying rocks are Paleozoic, meaning that they come from the time in which life proliferated. These specific 'Paleozoics' are from the Shadow Lake Formation which are Lower Ordovician in age (roughly 488-470 million years old).
Where am I?
You are on ON-36, just outside of Burleigh Falls, Ontario.
The Great Unconformity
The Great Unconformity marks the ancient core of of the continent Laurentia, which formed through a series of continentaly collisions and accretions. It separates Precambrian from Paleozoic Rocks.
Shield Rocks
These rocks are part of the 'Canadian Shield'. They are 1.1 billion year old Grenville gneisses, formed by squeezing of rocks under a mountain range.
Superposition
If we were to drive up this hill, we would see younger rocks, because in a flat-lying succession, younger rocks overlie older rocks. This is the law of superposition.
Reddish Colouration
The reddish colour comes from oxidation of iron-bearing minerals. It shows that at some point, these rocks were exposed to an oxygenated atmosphere and were weathered.
