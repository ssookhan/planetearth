Ripples
Just below this point, zoom in on the rockface to see the surface textures. Clear ripple marks are visible. Ripple marks are formed on the top of a bedding plane where water has flowed over sediment.
Jointing
The near-vertical cracks in the rock just below this point are 'joints' which are minor fractures across which there is no displacement. There are several joint sets in these outcrops in different orientations, and it is important to distinguish them from bedding planes (layers of sediment) and faults (fractures with displacement).
Underside of beds
On this side of the street, we can see the underside of beds and the overall dip of strata away from us. This plane is a bedding plane and the dip is roughly away from us.
Where am I?
You are on the Trans-Canada Highway, highway 17, just west of Desbarats Ontario, north of St. Joseph Island.
The Rocks
These rocks are metamorphosed sandstones of the Lorrain Formation. They are about 2.4 billion years old.
