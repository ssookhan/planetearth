Cold Currents
Oceans store and redistribute vast quantities of heat through Thermohaline Circulation (aka the Oceanic Conveyor Belt). Deep ocean circulation is controlled by density, which is a function of temperature and salinity. Changes to oceanic circulation, and thus how heat is redistributed from equator to poles can happen as a result of climate change and tectonic reconfiguration.

Atmospheric Composition
The composition of the atmosphere, and in particular the concentration of greenhouse gases like carbon dioxed is an important control on the long term temperature of the Earth's surface. A number of biogeochemical cycles influence the composition of the atmosphere, but we generally focus on carbon dioxide.
