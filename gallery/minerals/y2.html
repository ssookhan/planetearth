Sugilite - KNa2Fe3+2(Li3Si12)O30

Cyclosilicates

Habit: Light brownish yellow to bright magenta. Crystals, shaped like slender prisms, rare; commonly in interlocking aggregates of subhedral grains, compact to massive. Vitreous luster; transparent to translucent. White streak.


Environment: Found in strata-bound metamorphosed manganese deposits and peralkaline igneous rocks.


Etymology: Named for Professor Ken-ichi Sugi (1901-48), the Japanese petrologist who discovered the mineral.
